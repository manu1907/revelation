\chapter{Greek Text, Transliteration, And Gloss}

\section{Purpose}

This appendix gathers the working Greek text, transliteration, and deliberately literal gloss used throughout the book. It is not meant to replace a critical edition. It gives the reader a stable view of the reduced form on which the argument depends.

\section{Textual Note}

The working form used in the argument reads \textgreek{ὅσα εἶδεν} (\textit{hosa eiden}, \textit{as many things as he saw}) without \textgreek{τε} (\textit{te}, \textit{and}). Some printed traditions include the longer wording \textit{hosa te eiden}. The argument does not depend on importing that longer form. The shorter form still requires the reader to relate what John saw to the word of God and the testimony of Jesus Christ.
