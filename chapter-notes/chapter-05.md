# Chapter 5 Notes: Seven Core Phrases

## Purpose

Identify the seven core verbal heads and the phrases they head.

This chapter must persuade the reader that the list is not a handpicked set of interesting words. The core set is defined by grammatical and semantic features, then tested against the surface form.

## Central Claim

Revelation 1:1-2 contains seven core verbal heads:

```text
apokalypsis, edoken, deixai, esemanen, aposteilas, emartyresen, eiden
```

Six belong to the transmission nucleus. `eiden` is extranuclear because it names reception.

## Required Moves

1. Define "verbal head" in plain language.
2. Explain why a noun can carry verbal force.
3. Explain why `apokalypsis` is included.
4. Explain why `dei` and `genesthai` are excluded from the core set.
5. Identify the phrase headed by each core verbal head.
6. Show that the heads are occurrence terms.
7. Show the transmission/reception division.
8. Prepare the reader for the chiastic arrangement without arguing the full chiasm yet.

## Working Definitions

Core verbal head:

- A verbal head, including one verbal noun, that participates in forming a main non-modifying clause or clause-like unit in Revelation 1:1-3.

Non-core verbal head:

- A verbal head that belongs to a modifying clause rather than to the main clause-like sequence.

Transmission:

- A communicative or transfer event by which content, message, command, signification, or testimony moves from one participant to another.

Reception:

- The receiving side of the transmission structure, explicitly represented by `eiden` and argued through the larger testimony clause.

## The Seven Heads

| Head | Surface Form | Initial Category | Core Status | Working Reason |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `apokalypsis` | noun | communication / truth claim | Core | Verbal noun or action noun functioning as the first head in the transmission sequence. |
| `edoken` | finite verb | transfer | Core | Transitive occurrence verb in the main transmission sequence. |
| `deixai` | infinitive | communication / consciousness | Core | Purpose infinitive naming what the giving is for. |
| `esemanen` | finite verb | communication / consciousness | Core | Elliptical finite verb requiring supplied dependents. |
| `aposteilas` | participle | transfer | Core | Participial transfer verb that participates in the transmission sequence. |
| `emartyresen` | finite verb | communication / truth claim | Core | John bears witness to the named contents. |
| `eiden` | finite verb | reception | Core, extranuclear | Names John's visual reception and must be integrated with testimony. |

## Why `dei genesthai` Is Treated Separately

`ha dei genesthai` is essential to the verse, but it is not a core phrase in this analysis.

Reasons to test and document:

- `dei` heads a modifying relative clause attached to the things shown/signified.
- `genesthai` follows `dei` and is intransitive.
- The phrase identifies the content that must happen, not a separate step in the transmission sequence.
- Treating it as non-core keeps the system from confusing transmitted content with transmission acts.

## Evidence Needed

- Greek grammar support for verbal nouns or action nouns.
- Greek grammar support for infinitives, participles, and relative clauses.
- Lexical support for the semantic grouping of each head.
- Linguistic support for head/dependent language if Greek grammars do not provide enough.

## Warnings

- Do not imply that `apokalypsis` is formally a verb. It is a noun with verbal force in this analysis.
- Do not call `dei` unimportant. It is non-core, not irrelevant.
- Do not let "core" sound like a standard grammatical category unless the chapter defines it as this book's analytic term.
- Do not move to the chiasm before the reader understands why these seven heads have been selected.
