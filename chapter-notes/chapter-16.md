# Chapter 16 Notes: Objections And Limits

## Job Of The Chapter

Address the strongest objections and state the limits of the system before the conclusion.

## Core Argument

The familiar consensus is coherent but undergoverned. The reconstruction is complex because the Greek opening is compressed, not because the theory adds complexity for its own sake. The theory is falsifiable and limited: some claims are foundational, while others remain exposed and should be tested further.

## Required Moves

- Treat the mainstream linear chain fairly before critiquing it.
- Explain that the issue is governance, not grammatical impossibility.
- Answer the complexity objection by showing which problems the text itself creates.
- Test the chiasm by explanatory power rather than by pattern-labeling alone.
- State that `en tachei` may still carry temporal force; the system only rejects inherited default control by "soon."
- Distinguish foundational claims from more exposed claims.
- End by making the final summary necessary.

## Avoid

- Do not mock consensus or translators.
- Do not claim the reconstruction is obvious or simple.
- Do not overstate `en tachei` by making "soon" impossible.
- Do not pretend every conclusion has equal certainty.
