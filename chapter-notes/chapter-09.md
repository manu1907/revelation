# Chapter 9 Notes: What Is The Apokalypsis?

## Job Of The Chapter

Reassess `apokalypsis` after the expanded form has been presented.

## Core Argument

`Revelation` fails as the controlling English rendering because it makes the opening term sound like a title, foregrounds hiddenness too quickly, and encourages readers to identify the disclosure with the written book.

## Preferred Rendering

Use `disclosure` as the controlled working rendering:

- It preserves eventive force.
- It does not require hiddenness to be the controlling idea.
- It does not collapse the disclosed entity into the canonical book.
- It fits the A / A' pair with John's testimony.

## Required Moves

- Test `revelation`, `unveiling`, and `disclosure`.
- Explain why the A / A' pair governs the content side of `apokalypsis`.
- Preserve the ambiguity of `Iesou Christou` for Chapter 10.
- Distinguish the disclosure from the written book.
- End by making the agency/recipient question necessary.

## Avoid

- Do not say `revelation` is lexically impossible.
- Do not decide the genitive of `Iesou Christou` by shortcut.
- Do not equate the book with the disclosure.
- Do not overargue `ton logon tou theou` and `ten martyrian Iesou Christou`; Chapter 14 handles them directly.
