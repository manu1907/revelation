# Chapter 13 Notes: Shown And Signified

## Job Of The Chapter

Reassess the relation between `deixai` and `esemanen` after `en tachei` has been located inside the C / C' pair.

## Core Argument

The things that must happen in speed are both shown and signified to God's servants. Showing presents or displays the content; signifying communicates it through signs, indications, or marked forms requiring interpretation. The two verbs share content and recipients, but they are not synonyms.

## Required Moves

- Introduce Greek script, transliteration, and gloss for `deixai` and `esemanen`.
- Distinguish showing from mere information transfer.
- Explain why `esemanen` should not be flattened into "made known."
- Use the C / C' pair to argue that the same content is shown and signified.
- Connect signifying to the visionary and symbolic mode of the book of Revelation.
- Avoid making symbolic communication unreal or arbitrary.
- End by making John's testimony the next necessary question.

## Avoid

- Do not detach signifying from the content shown to the servants.
- Do not imply symbols are unreal.
- Do not reduce signifying to a simple one-to-one code.
- Do not fully argue John's testimony; save it for Chapter 14.
