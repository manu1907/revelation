# Chapter 14 Notes: John's Testimony

## Job Of The Chapter

Clarify what John writes and what his testimony contains.

## Core Argument

Revelation 1:2 is the reception-side counterpart to the opening transmission structure. John testified to the word of God, the testimony of Jesus Christ, and what he saw. Seeing is explicit in `eiden`; the heard/verbal dimension is argued from `ton logon tou theou`, `ten martyrian Iesou Christou`, and the broader communication model, not from silently importing `te` into the critical-text base.

## Required Moves

- Present the surface form of Revelation 1:2.
- Explain the variant issue: `hosa eiden` versus `hosa te eiden`.
- Analyze `ton logon tou theou` as a communicative object of John's testimony.
- Analyze `ten martyrian Iesou Christou` without simply copying the decision made for `apokalypsis Iesou Christou`.
- Analyze `hosa eiden` as the extranuclear reception element.
- Show how the final clause relates to the A / A' pair: `apokalypsis` and `emartyresen`.
- Explain why John's testimony is not simply "he wrote down the book of Revelation."
- Connect John's testimony to the larger claim that the disclosure is not identical with the written book.

## Avoid

- Do not let "what he saw" erase the word/testimony objects.
- Do not let the heard/saw claim depend on the TR reading alone.
- Do not treat `ton logon tou theou` and `ten martyrian Iesou Christou` as ornamental religious language.
- Do not force one genitive label onto `ten martyrian Iesou Christou` before the structure has done its work.
