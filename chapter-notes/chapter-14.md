# Chapter 14 Notes: John's Testimony

## Purpose

Clarify what John writes and what his testimony contains.

This chapter must show that Revelation 1:2 is not an afterthought. It is the reception-side counterpart to the opening transmission structure.

## Central Claim

John testified to the word of God, the testimony of Jesus Christ, and what he saw. The chapter will argue that this means John testified to what he heard and saw, but it must do so without silently importing `te` into the critical-text base.

## Required Moves

1. Present the surface form of Revelation 1:2.
2. Explain the variant issue: `hosa eiden` versus `hosa te eiden`.
3. Analyze `ton logon tou theou`.
4. Analyze `ten martyrian iesou christou`.
5. Analyze `hosa/eiden` as the extranuclear reception element.
6. Show how the final clause relates to the A/A' pair: `apokalypsis` and `emartyresen`.
7. Explain why John's testimony is not simply "he wrote down the book of Revelation."
8. Connect John's testimony to the larger claim that the so-called revelation is not identical with the book.

## Evidence Needed

- Critical text and apparatus for the presence/absence of `te`.
- Translation comparison rows for the final clause.
- Commentary evidence on whether verse 2 summarizes the book, John's testimony, the contents of the disclosure, or all of these.
- Lexical and usage evidence for `logos`, `martyria`, `martyreo`, and `horao/eidon`.
- Internal evidence from Revelation for hearing and seeing as modes of John's reception.

## Warnings

- Do not let the phrase "what he saw" erase the word/testimony objects.
- Do not let the heard/saw claim depend on the TR reading alone.
- Do not treat `ton logon tou theou` and `ten martyrian iesou christou` as ornamental religious language.
- Do not decide `ten martyrian iesou christou` by simply copying the decision made for `apokalypsis iesou christou`; the repeated genitive phrase is evidence, not a shortcut.
