# Chapter 15 Notes: A Different Interpretive Frame

## Job Of The Chapter

Explain how the reconstructed opening affects the reading frame for the rest of the book of Revelation.

## Core Argument

The opening changes how readers should approach futurity, novelty, sequence, and multiple interpretations. Futurity remains real but is embedded in servant obedience. Novelty exists inside continuity with the word of God and testimony of Jesus Christ. Sequence matters, but surface order is not automatically chronology. Multiple interpretations arise because the disclosure is both shown and signified.

## Required Moves

- Reconsider futurity without denying that the book concerns things that must happen.
- Reconsider novelty without reducing the book to either new secrets or recycled symbols.
- Reconsider sequence in light of the non-linear opening structure.
- Explain why visionary/signifying communication produces layered interpretations.
- State the new reading frame without pretending it answers every later exegetical question.
- End by making objections and limits the next necessary question.

## Avoid

- Do not claim futurity is irrelevant.
- Do not force the whole book into one interpretive grid.
- Do not make symbolism unreal or arbitrary.
- Do not overclaim that Revelation 1:1--3 solves every later interpretive problem.
