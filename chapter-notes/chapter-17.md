# Chapter 17 Notes: Conclusion

## Job Of The Chapter

State the result plainly and invite the reader to reread the book of Revelation from the opening's own constraints.

## Core Argument

Revelation 1:1-3 is not a loose preface but a governed disclosure-and-testimony system. The conclusion summarizes the method, lists the major interpretive changes, and frames rereading as disciplined attention to showing, signifying, servants, testimony, and what is written.

## Required Moves

- Summarize the chiastic transmission system without rearguing every detail.
- Summarize the major changes in meaning produced by the system.
- Keep exposed claims marked as conclusions of the analysis rather than self-evident facts.
- Invite rereading the book of Revelation from the opening's constraints.
- End with the opening itself as the guide for further reading.

## Avoid

- Do not introduce new technical claims.
- Do not imply every later interpretive problem is solved.
- Do not overstate certainty on the more exposed conclusions.
- Do not end with a generic devotional summary detached from the argument.
