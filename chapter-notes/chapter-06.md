# Chapter 6 Notes: Ellipsis As Evidence

## Job Of The Chapter

Move ellipsis from a nuisance to evidence that must be governed.

## Core Argument

Revelation 1:1-3 contains compressed expressions that require supplied or related dependents. The problem is not that interpreters supply material, but that supplied material must be licensed by a stated recoverability structure rather than by the familiar linear chain.

## Required Moves

- Define ellipsis in accessible language.
- Explain recoverability as the central issue.
- Identify the clearest ellipses in `esemanen` and `aposteilas`.
- Show related compression in `deixai`, `apokalypsis Iesou Christou`, and the final testimony clause.
- Compare the common translation/commentary supply pattern without caricaturing it.
- Make Chapter 7 necessary by asking whether the passage provides a structure that governs what may be supplied.

## Avoid

- Do not present the expanded form.
- Do not argue the chiasm yet.
- Do not claim that all compression is ellipsis in the same narrow sense.
- Do not treat mainstream supplies as foolish; the point is under-governed supply.
