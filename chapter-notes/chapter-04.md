# Chapter 4 Notes: The Reduced Form

## Job Of The Chapter

Present Revelation 1:1-3 in its compact Greek surface form before any expanded reconstruction is introduced.

## Core Argument

The reduced form is not a defective form or a translation artifact. It is the Greek surface form itself, and its compression is evidence that must be governed rather than smoothed away.

## Required Moves

- Present the Greek text of Revelation 1:1-3.
- Give a deliberately literal transliteration and gloss.
- Preserve ambiguity in `Iesou Christou`, `auto`, `deixai`, `esemanen`, `aposteilas`, the servants, and `en tachei`.
- Show where smooth translations supply relations or dependents.
- Make Chapter 5 necessary by asking which elements should count as structurally central.

## Textual Note

Use the common critical form of Revelation 1:2 with `hosa eiden`, not the TR-style `hosa te eiden`.

The `te` issue should be handled later; Chapter 4 should only note that the present argument is not silently relying on the longer form.

## Avoid

- Do not present the expanded form.
- Do not argue the chiasm.
- Do not make translations sound dishonest; the point is that translations must decide.
- Do not treat verse 3 as irrelevant, but keep the densest analysis on verses 1-2.
