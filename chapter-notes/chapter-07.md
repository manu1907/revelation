# Chapter 7 Notes: The Chiasm

## Purpose

Present the six-phrase chiastic arrangement and explain how it constrains interpretation.

This chapter must make the chiasm do disciplined work. It cannot merely display an attractive pattern.

## Central Claim

The six transmission heads form the sequence:

```text
A   apokalypsis
B   edoken
C   deixai
C'  esemanen
B'  aposteilas
A'  emartyresen
```

This lexical chiasm percolates upward into the phrases headed by those words:

```text
A   ἀποκάλυψις
B   ἰησοῦ χριστοῦ ἣν ἔδωκεν αὐτῷ ὁ θεός
C   δεῖξαι τοῖς δούλοις αὐτοῦ ἃ δεῖ γενέσθαι ἐν τάχει
C'  καὶ ἐσήμανεν
B'  ἀποστείλας διὰ τοῦ ἀγγέλου αὐτοῦ τῷ δούλῳ αὐτοῦ ἰωάννῃ
A'  ὃς ἐμαρτύρησεν τὸν λόγον τοῦ θεοῦ καὶ τὴν μαρτυρίαν ἰησοῦ χριστοῦ
```

## Required Moves

1. Define chiasm as inverted parallelism.
2. Explain why the proposed structure begins at the lexical level.
3. Identify the semantic basis for each pair.
4. Show how the lexical pattern percolates to the phrasal level.
5. Explain what a chiastic pair can and cannot supply.
6. Test each pair for symmetry, contrast, complementarity, and sequence.
7. Show why `eiden` is outside the chiastic nucleus but still structurally necessary.

## Pair Analysis

### A / A': `apokalypsis` and `emartyresen`

Shared category:

- communication / truth claim.

Questions:

- What content belongs to `apokalypsis`?
- What content belongs to `emartyresen`?
- How does the final testimony clause supply the content side of the opening head?
- Why does this pair exclude "revelation" as the controlling English equivalent for `apokalypsis`?
- What chronological relation exists between the transmitting-side disclosure and John's witness?

### B / B': `edoken` and `aposteilas`

Shared category:

- transfer.

Questions:

- Who gives?
- Who receives?
- What is given?
- Who sends?
- What or whom is sent?
- How does the pair contrast direct and indirect transfer?
- Why does sending precede giving even though the surface order places `edoken` before `aposteilas`?

### C / C': `deixai` and `esemanen`

Shared category:

- communication / consciousness.

Questions:

- Who shows?
- Who signifies?
- What is shown?
- What is signified?
- To whom are the things shown and signified?
- How does `deixai` supply the missing dependents of `esemanen`?
- What chronological or logical relation exists between showing and signifying?

## Rule For Reinstatement

A chiastic pair can supply missing dependents when:

- the paired heads share a defined semantic category,
- the surface form leaves a dependent unexpressed,
- the corresponding phrase expresses the missing dependent,
- the resulting reconstruction is more economical than introducing a new participant or object,
- the reconstruction can be checked against the rest of the transmission/reception model.

The rule does not allow arbitrary additions. It licenses recoverable dependents only.

## Evidence Needed

- Sources defining chiasm and inverted parallelism.
- Sources warning against uncontrolled chiasm detection.
- Lexical evidence for the semantic grouping of the heads.
- Commentary evidence showing whether existing interpreters notice any ellipsis or structural symmetry.

## Warnings

- Do not treat chiasm as decoration.
- Do not claim perfect symmetry where the structure contains holdouts or misfits.
- Do not hide the fact that the proposed chiasm is original if no major commentary identifies it.
- Do not let the structure become unfalsifiable. State what would count against it.
