# Chapter 2 Notes: The Trouble With Plausible Readings

## Job Of The Chapter

Show that the mainstream readings of Revelation 1:1-3 are often plausible, but plausibility alone is not enough.

## Core Problems

- `apokalypsis iesou christou`: source, subject, possession, or content?
- `hen edoken auto ho theos`: who or what is `auto`?
- `deixai`: who is the subject of the showing?
- `tois doulois autou`: whose servants, and which servants?
- `ha dei genesthai en tachei`: soon, speedily, or otherwise?
- `esemanen`: what was signified, by whom, and to whom?
- `aposteilas`: what was sent, or who was sent, and how does the participle function?

## Developmental Point

This chapter should repeatedly say: "This reading is possible. The question is why it should be chosen."

## Evidence To Gather

- Examples of commentators solving each ambiguity.
- Examples of translations hiding each ambiguity.
- A table showing that interpretive choices can vary independently unless governed by a larger structure.

## Risk

This chapter could become too negative. It should make the reader feel the need for a better system, not merely distrust experts.
