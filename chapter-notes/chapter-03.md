# Chapter 3 Notes: What Counts As An Interpretation System?

## Job Of The Chapter

Define the standards the book will use before the Greek surface form is examined in detail.

## Core Argument

Plausibility is not enough. An interpretation system must move from grammatical possibility and contextual plausibility to system-governed conclusions.

## Standards Introduced

- Predictability: similar evidence should produce similar interpretive moves.
- Consistency: the method should not shift without reason from phrase to phrase.
- Economy: the interpretation should use what the passage supplies before importing new assumptions.
- Exclusion: the system must be able to rule out plausible readings, not merely list them.
- Testability: the system must state what would count against it.

## Methodological Order

1. Local Greek.
2. Ellipsis.
3. Structure.
4. Intertextuality.
5. Theology.

## Developmental Function

This chapter ends Part I. It should make Chapter 4 necessary by arguing that the reduced Greek surface form must now be examined before the expanded form can be presented.

## Avoid

- Do not make the system sound perfectly symmetrical.
- Do not introduce the full chiasm too early.
- Do not present the expanded form in this chapter.
- Do not make the method sound anti-theological; theology is delayed, not dismissed.
