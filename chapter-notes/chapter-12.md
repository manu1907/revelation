# Chapter 12 Notes: Soon, Speedily, Or In Speed?

## Job Of The Chapter

Reassess `en tachei` after the servant question has been located inside the disclosure system.

## Core Argument

`En tachei` should not be inherited as simply "soon." The phrase belongs to `ha dei genesthai`, the content shown and signified to God's servants. "Soon" remains possible, but the structure does not require it as the controlling sense. "In speed" is the control gloss, with "speedily" as the smoother working option.

## Required Moves

- Present the major options: soon/shortly, quickly/speedily, and the control gloss "in speed."
- Show what each option assumes about the events.
- Distinguish temporal nearness from speed or manner without pretending they are unrelated.
- Emphasize `dei genesthai`: necessity and happening belong to the disclosed content.
- Test the phrase inside the C / C' pair.
- Connect the phrase to servant reception rather than curiosity about a schedule.
- End by making showing/signifying the next necessary question.

## Avoid

- Do not claim `soon` is grammatically impossible.
- Do not use "speedily" to erase all temporal force.
- Do not decide every timing problem in the book of Revelation.
- Do not let chronology control the communication structure before Chapter 13 handles showing/signifying.
