# Research Log

## Research Policy

Each claim should be tracked as one of the following:

- Textual: based on the Greek text.
- Lexical: based on word meaning or usage.
- Grammatical: based on syntax, morphology, or discourse structure.
- Structural: based on arrangement, chiasm, ellipsis, or parallelism.
- Intertextual: based on links to other biblical texts.
- Historical: based on reception, translation history, or commentator history.
- Theological: based on doctrinal coherence or theological implications.

The book should not let theological claims do the work that grammatical or structural claims are supposed to do.

## Claim Inventory

### Claim: `apokalypsis iesou christou` is grammatically ambiguous.

Status: Established in principle; to document with sources.

Evidence needed:

- Grammars or studies on genitive constructions.
- Commentators and translators who classify the genitive differently.
- Examples of `apokalypsis` plus genitive where the genitive is subjective, objective, possessive, or content-related.
- Authors who explicitly acknowledge "about Jesus Christ" or an objective/content-related reading as plausible.

Analytical note:

The book does not need to prove merely that "about Jesus Christ" is possible. That point is grammatical and already supported by multiple authors. The book must show why a rule-ordered interpretation system validates one plausible option and excludes others.

### Claim: The system excludes translating `apokalypsis` as "revelation."

Status: To document.

Evidence needed:

- Lexical entries for `apokalypsis`.
- Translation history.
- Semantic difference between "revelation," "disclosure," "uncovering," and "unveiling."
- Evidence that "revelation" entails that the thing revealed was previously hidden, unknown, or concealed.
- Structural evidence from the chiasm comparing and contrasting `apokalypsis` with `emartyresen`.
- Explanation of how `apokalypsis` and `emartyresen` do not require previously hidden, unknown, or concealed content, while one does the contrary of what the other does.
