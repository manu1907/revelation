# Evidence Matrix

## Purpose

This file defines the evidence table used to compare translations, commentaries, and Greek text witnesses. Its job is to show what mainstream interpretation actually is, where it is created by translation or commentary, and where the proposed system explains the surface form differently.

The table must track the full movement of Revelation 1:1-3, including the final testimony clause. The book's argument depends not only on `apokalypsis`, `auto`, `deixai`, `esemanen`, `aposteilas`, servants, and `en tachei`, but also on:

- `ton logon tou theou`,
- `ten martyrian iesou christou`,
- `hosa eiden` / `hosa te eiden`,
- whether John testified to what he heard and saw,
- whether the source treats verse 2 as a summary of the whole book, the content of the `apokalypsis`, or John's act of testimony.

## Evidence Categories

Use the following source categories:

- Greek edition.
- English translation.
- Technical commentary.
- Pastoral/exegetical commentary.
- Study Bible or translation note.
- Monograph or article.

## Core Questions

Each source should be checked against these questions:

- Does the source treat Revelation 1:1-3 as a linear communication chain?
- Does it acknowledge ellipsis?
- Does it identify any structural symmetry?
- Does it supply missing subjects, objects, or intermediaries?
- Does it present interpretive decisions as if they were simple translation?
- Does it distinguish Greek possibility from exegetical conclusion?

## Initial Greek Text Witness Notes

Current working observation:

- NA28/UBS5, SBLGNT, THGNT, ECM, and the Robinson-Pierpont Byzantine text appear to read `hosa eiden` without `te`.
- Textus Receptus editions include `te`, reading `hosa te eiden`.

Research status:

- Verify directly in NA28/UBS5 apparatus.
- Verify THGNT and RP readings from printed or authoritative digital sources.
- Record whether any modern translation is based on a Greek text with `te`.

Interpretive importance:

- If `te` is present, it can more visibly coordinate what John saw with the preceding testimony material.
- If `te` is absent, the relation between `ton logon tou theou`, `ten martyrian iesou christou`, and `hosa eiden` still must be explained; the absence of `te` does not remove the question.
- The book's claim that John testified to what he heard and saw must be framed in relation to this textual issue.

## Immediate Warnings

- Do not overquote copyrighted translations or commentaries. Record interpretive decisions mostly in paraphrase.
- Do not call a reading "mainstream" merely because one major source adopts it.
- Do not treat the KJV/TR `te` reading as irrelevant simply because NA28 omits it.
- Do not treat the NA28 omission of `te` as fatal to the heard/saw claim without first analyzing the relation of the final clause.
