# Final Testimony And `te`

## Purpose

This file develops the argument around Revelation 1:2:

```text
ὃς ἐμαρτύρησεν τὸν λόγον τοῦ θεοῦ καὶ τὴν μαρτυρίαν ἰησοῦ χριστοῦ ὅσα εἶδεν
```

and the Textus Receptus-type expansion:

```text
ὃς ἐμαρτύρησεν τὸν λόγον τοῦ θεοῦ καὶ τὴν μαρτυρίαν ἰησοῦ χριστοῦ ὅσα τε εἶδεν
```

The book's working claim is that John testified to what he heard and saw. That claim must be argued from the surface form, the structure, and the transmission/reception model, not smuggled in through a preferred English paraphrase.

## Textual Issue

Working observation:

- Critical-text editions used as the project's base text appear to read `hosa eiden`, without `te`.
- TR-type editions read `hosa te eiden`.

This difference matters because `te` can make coordination more visible. The absence of `te`, however, does not settle the relation of `hosa eiden` to `ton logon tou theou` and `ten martyrian iesou christou`.

Required verification:

- NA28 apparatus for Revelation 1:2.
- UBS5 apparatus for Revelation 1:2.
- SBLGNT and THGNT wording.
- Textus Receptus edition used in the comparison table.
- Whether major English translations follow a text with or without `te`.

## Key Constituents

### `ton logon tou theou`

Working function:

- This is one object of John's testimony.
- It belongs to the truth-claim side of the structure.
- It naturally supports an auditory dimension because a `logos` is communicable speech or message, but that must be documented from usage and context.

Questions:

- Is the phrase source-oriented: word from God?
- Is it content-oriented: the message belonging to God?
- Does it summarize what God has given, shown, and signified?
- Does it contrast with or correspond to `apokalypsis` in A?

### `ten martyrian iesou christou`

Working function:

- This is a second object of John's testimony.
- It must be analyzed in relation to both the opening `iesou christou` and the testimony verb `emartyresen`.
- It may help explain why `apokalypsis iesou christou` is not decided by grammar alone.

Questions:

- Is this testimony borne by Jesus Christ, testimony about Jesus Christ, testimony belonging to Jesus Christ, or a layered relation?
- How does this phrase compare with `apokalypsis iesou christou`?
- If the angel is Jesus Christ in the proposed system, how does that affect the testimony phrase?
- Does this phrase name what John heard, what John saw, or both?

### `hosa eiden` / `hosa te eiden`

Working function:

- `eiden` is the extranuclear reception element.
- It confirms that the transmission structure reaches a recipient.
- It must be connected to the things shown and signified, not treated as a decorative ending.

Questions:

- Does `hosa eiden` summarize both preceding objects?
- Does it stand in apposition to the word of God and testimony of Jesus Christ?
- Does it specify the visual mode of John's reception?
- Does it add a visual component to an already implied auditory component?

## Relation To The Chiastic Nucleus

The A/A' pair is:

```text
A   apokalypsis
A'  hos emartyresen ton logon tou theou kai ten martyrian iesou christou
```

Both heads belong to communication and truth claims. The pair requires comparison and contrast:

- `apokalypsis` is the act or event of disclosure/testimony-like communication from the transmitting side.
- `emartyresen` is John's act of witness from the receiving side after transmission reaches him.
- Neither term by itself requires that the content was previously hidden, unknown, or concealed.
- The pair helps exclude "revelation" as the controlling English equivalent for `apokalypsis`.

## Working Argument For Heard And Saw

The argument should not depend on the TR `te`. It should proceed in this order:

1. John testifies to named communicative contents: `ton logon tou theou` and `ten martyrian iesou christou`.
2. Those contents belong to a transmission structure that includes giving, showing, signifying, and sending.
3. `eiden` names visual reception and stands outside the chiastic nucleus as reception evidence.
4. The word/testimony language supplies the auditory or message dimension that `eiden` alone does not name.
5. The TR `te` may make the coordination more explicit, but the base-text argument must work without it.

## Drafting Warning

Do not write as though `te` is in the base text unless the chapter is explicitly discussing the TR reading. When drafting from the critical text, say that John's testimony includes the word of God, the testimony of Jesus Christ, and what he saw; then argue that this amounts to what he heard and saw through the larger communicative structure.
